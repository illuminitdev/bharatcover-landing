Common Questions

Everything you need to know before you buy.

For Bharat Arogya Individual, the minimum sum insured is ₹1 Lakh. For Bharat Suraksha Accident, you can start from ₹1 Lakh of accidental cover. The Bharat Suraksha Daily Cash plan provides a fixed daily benefit of ₹500 or ₹1,000 per day rather than a lump sum insured.
Yes. In fact, we recommend combining plans for maximum protection. A common combination is Bharat Arogya Individual (for hospitalisation costs) + Bharat Suraksha Daily Cash (for daily cash benefit) + Bharat Suraksha Accident (for accidental death and disability). The daily cash benefit is paid regardless of any other insurance you hold.
No. Bharat Suraksha Accident has zero waiting period. Cover is active from the moment your policy start date begins. There is no initial waiting period, no pre-existing disease exclusion (accidents are not pre-existing conditions), and no specific illness waiting period. You are covered from Day 1.
Co-pay means you share a fixed percentage of every claim with the insurer. The Bharat Arogya Family Floater has a 10% co-pay on all claims, meaning if your total claim is ₹50,000, you pay ₹5,000 and the insurer pays ₹45,000. The Bharat Arogya Individual plan does not have a mandatory co-pay. Co-pay is one of the reasons the Family Floater premium is lower than buying separate individual policies for each family member.
New family members can typically be added at the time of policy renewal. Mid-term additions may be allowed for newborns within a certain number of days of birth — please check with your advisor for the specific insurer's rules. The floater plan supports up to 2 adults and 2 children. If you need to add a member mid-term, speak to your BharatCover advisor who can help you with an endorsement request.

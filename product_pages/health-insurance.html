Frequently Asked Questions

What does 'co-pay' mean in this plan?
↓
Co-pay (or co-payment) means you share a fixed percentage of the admissible claim with the insurer. Under this plan, you pay 10% of the admissible claim amount while the insurance company pays the remaining 90%. For example, on a ₹50,000 claim, you bear ₹5,000 and the insurer pays ₹45,000. Co-pay does not apply to policy premiums — only to actual claims.
Which hospitals are in the cashless network?
↓
Magma General Insurance has empanelled 10,000+ hospitals across India for cashless treatment. You can search for network hospitals on the insurer's website or through the BharatCover portal. Always confirm cashless availability with the hospital before admission for planned procedures. For emergencies, you can seek treatment first and file for reimbursement if the hospital is not in-network.
Is day care treatment covered?
↓
Yes. The Bharat Arogya Individual plan covers over 540 day care procedures that do not require 24-hour hospitalisation due to advances in medical technology. This includes cataract surgery, chemotherapy, dialysis, angiography, lithotripsy, and many other procedures. The full list is available in the policy document and subject to standard waiting periods.
What is room rent capping and how does it affect my claim?
↓
Room rent capping limits the amount the insurer pays per day for your hospital room. Under this plan, the limit is 2% of the Sum Insured per day for a normal ward and 4% for ICU. If you choose a room that exceeds this cap, proportionate deductions apply — meaning other associated expenses like surgeon fees and nursing charges are also reduced proportionately. Choosing a room within the capped limit avoids this deduction.
Can I port my existing health policy to Bharat Arogya?
↓
Yes, portability is available as per IRDAI guidelines. You can port your existing health insurance policy to Bharat Arogya Individual and retain the waiting period credits accumulated under your previous policy. Portability requests must be initiated at least 45 days before your policy renewal date. Speak with a BharatCover advisor to understand how your existing waiting periods carry over.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I add a newborn child to my existing floater policy?
↓
Yes, you can add a newborn child to your Family Floater policy mid-term. You must notify the insurer within 90 days of the child's birth and pay the additional pro-rata premium. The child will be covered from the date of addition. Speak with your BharatCover advisor to process the endorsement quickly and ensure no gap in coverage.
What happens if the full sum insured is used by one family member?
↓
In a floater policy, the sum insured is shared across all members. If one member exhausts the entire sum insured, remaining family members will have no coverage for that policy year. It is advisable to choose a higher sum insured for floater plans to avoid this risk. Consider a ₹5L or higher SI for families, especially if elderly members are included. You can also add a Super Top-Up plan for additional buffer coverage.
Is the pre-existing disease waiting period per person or for the whole family?
↓
The PED waiting period applies per individual insured member, not for the whole family collectively. Each member must complete their respective waiting period before PED-related claims are admitted. Under this floater plan, the PED waiting period is 12 months — shorter than the 24-month period on the Individual plan. Waiting periods carried over from a previous policy via portability can reduce this further.
Can I choose different sum insured for different family members?
↓
No — under a floater policy, a single sum insured is shared among all covered members. You cannot assign different sum insured levels to individual family members within the same floater plan. If you need customised cover levels for different members, you would need to take separate individual policies for each person instead of a floater.
What is the age limit for children covered under the floater?
↓
Children can be covered under the Family Floater from 91 days (with newborn addition endorsement) up to 25 years of age, provided they are financially dependent on the policyholder. Once a child turns 26 or becomes financially independent, they will need to be removed from the floater and can purchase their own Individual health policy. Contact your advisor at renewal to review dependent member status.

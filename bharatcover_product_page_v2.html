What's Covered

Core benefits included in all Personal Accident plans

Accidental Death (AD)

Full sum insured paid to nominee in the event of death caused directly by an accident.

Permanent Total Disability (PTD)

100% sum insured paid for loss of both limbs, both eyes, or one limb + one eye due to accident.

Permanent Partial Disability (PPD)

Proportionate benefit as per the Schedule of Benefits — loss of specific limbs or sensory organs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a waiting period for Personal Accident cover?
↓
No — Personal Accident policies have no waiting period. Coverage begins from Day 1 of the policy inception date. There are no exclusions for pre-existing conditions in PA policies since it only covers accidents, not illnesses.
Can I buy this for my family members too?
↓
Yes. You can purchase individual PA policies for each family member — spouse, children, and parents — with separate sum insured for each. Group PA (for families) is also available with discounted premiums.
What counts as an "accident" under this policy?
↓
An accident is defined as a sudden, unexpected, external, and involuntary event. This includes road accidents, falls, burns, drowning, and other physical mishaps. It does not include self-inflicted injuries or death due to illness.
What is PTD vs PPD benefit?
↓
PTD (Permanent Total Disability) pays 100% of sum insured for complete loss of use of both hands, both legs, or combination of limb and eye. PPD (Permanent Partial Disability) pays a proportionate percentage as per the insurer's Schedule of Benefits — typically 10% to 50% depending on the body part or organ affected.

What's Covered

Benefits paid per day of hospitalisation — directly to you

Hospital Daily Cash

A fixed cash benefit for each day you are hospitalised, for a maximum of 30 days per policy year.

ICU Daily Cash — Double Benefit

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I claim Daily Cash on top of my health insurance?
↓
Yes! Hospital Daily Cash is a benefit policy, not an indemnity policy. It pays a fixed cash amount regardless of your actual hospitalisation bills. You can claim it in addition to your health insurance reimbursement — there's no duplication conflict under IRDAI guidelines.
Is a minimum hospitalisation duration required?
↓
Yes — a minimum of 24 consecutive hours of in-patient hospitalisation is required for the daily cash benefit to be triggered. Daycare procedures and OPD visits are not covered.
Does the ICU benefit automatically double?
↓
Yes. The ICU daily benefit is exactly double the ward benefit. If you are in ICU, the higher amount is paid automatically — no separate claim or documentation is required beyond the ICU admission record on the discharge summary.
Can I buy both Personal Accident and Daily Cash together?
↓
Absolutely — and we recommend it. Personal Accident covers accidental events (death/disability), while Hospital Daily Cash covers illness-related hospitalisations. Together they provide comprehensive income protection. We offer combined packages at discounted premiums.
